About Us

Nurturing Tomorrow's Forest Today

Who We Are

The Oloolua Forest Seedling User Group, operating under the Oloolua Community Forest Association, stands as a pivotal force in forest conservation and community development. This dedicated group of local community members specializes in tree nursery management and seedling production, playing a crucial role in maintaining the forest's biodiversity.

User Rights and Privileges

Under their agreement with the Kenya Forest Service, the group enjoys several key rights within the forest ecosystem:

  • Access to designated areas for establishing tree nurseries
  • Permission to collect seeds from the forest for propagation
  • Right to sell seedlings to conservation projects and community members
  • Access to water sources for nursery irrigation
  • Permission to conduct training sessions within forest grounds

Core Activities

Seed Collection and Processing

Members expertly collect seeds from indigenous tree species, ensuring proper timing and handling. They maintain detailed records of seed sources and processing methods to guarantee quality.

Nursery Management

The group maintains a well-organized nursery facility where they:

  • Prepare potting soil using local materials
  • Propagate both indigenous and exotic tree species
  • Monitor seedling growth and health
  • Implement pest management strategies
  • Practice water conservation techniques

Training and Knowledge Sharing

Experienced members regularly conduct training sessions for:

  • New group members
  • Local community members
  • School groups
  • Other CFAs interested in nursery establishment

Commercial Activities

The group generates sustainable income through:

  • Selling tree seedlings to conservation projects
  • Providing seedlings for forest restoration
  • Supporting local farmers with agroforestry species
  • Supplying ornamental trees to urban developers

Impact and Achievements

5,000+ Seedlings Annually
20+ Indigenous Species
100% Local Employment

Join Our Cause

Through their dedicated efforts, the Oloolua Forest Seedling User Group not only contributes to forest conservation but also demonstrates how community-based initiatives can successfully combine environmental stewardship with economic empowerment.
