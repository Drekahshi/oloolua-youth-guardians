Our Activities

Bringing together community, conservation, and innovation to create lasting green value

As a CFA-approved group inside Oloolua Forest we run every activity with three simultaneous goals: protect the forest, empower the community, and generate verifiable investable green impact.

Seedling Production & Nursery Management

We propagate indigenous, medicinal, and agroforestry tree species covering the full lifecycle: seed collection, soil preparation, germination, shade-net cultivation, pest control, and hardening-off before field deployment. Every seedling is logged for the Hedera-based carbon data trail.

Forest Conservation & Restoration

  • Active Restoration: Structured planting drives in degraded patches with species matched to soil and ecological fit.
  • Forest Clean-Ups: Regular litter and invasive-species removal.
  • Biodiversity Monitoring: Canopy cover tracking, invasive species ID, documentation of returning birds and understory plants feeding ESG reports and biodiversity credits.
  • Wildlife Corridors & Water Catchment: Rehabilitation of animal movement paths and forested slopes regulating Oloolua's streams.
  • Jaza Miti: 10,000 verified trees/year scaling to 50,000 by 2028 — every tree geo-tagged and recorded on Hedera.

Green Space Development

  • Mapped nature trails with interpretive ecological signage
  • Eco-gazebos, hammock zones, shaded seating & resting areas
  • Treehouse — bird-watching and canopy viewing platform
  • Forest Library — quiet research and reading space for students
  • Children's Zone — nature-themed safe play area for families
  • Art House — creative hub for workshops and cultural production

Camping Facility

10-tent immersive forest camping with bedding, upgraded ablutions, fire pit, solar lighting, and trained youth staff. Target: 60 camper-nights/month at KES 2,500/night. 2026 Revenue: KES 180,000 | ROI: 71%

Clean Energy Integration

Portable lithium battery energy system replacing fuel generators across the entire forest — eliminating noise and emissions while enabling night events, extended hours, and clean visitor experiences. Estimated deployment: KES 150,000–300,000.

Event Hosting & Corporate Packages

Our highest-ROI activity at 733% — full PA system, event décor, and catering equipment on-site. Corporate retreats: KES 10,000–25,000+ per event. School eco-days, forest runs, community gatherings. 2026 Revenue: KES 300,000

Forest Recreation & Games Hub

Weekend Uji Power Programme

Every weekend OFYG runs a community nutrition station providing fortified uji to children and families — free or at minimal cost. The programme ensures every family has a welcome in Oloolua regardless of income, building the deep community ownership that protects the forest long-term.

Wellness & Community Engagement

  • Health walks, morning runs, yoga and meditation in forest clearings
  • Outdoor storytelling, nature therapy circles, and breathing workshops
  • Kids' Environmental Days — scavenger hunts, seed planting, guided bird spotting
  • Night events powered by our clean lithium battery system

Conservation Data Tracking — Powered by Hedera

We solve the verification problem in conservation finance with Hedera Hashgraph — tamper-proof, enterprise-grade, lower energy than traditional blockchains.

  1. Plant — GPS, species & date logged via mobile in real time
  2. Validate — Hedera Guardian applies our published policy & triggers peer review
  3. Commit — Verified record written permanently to the Hedera network
  4. Credential — W3C Verifiable Credential issued per validated batch
  5. Monetise — Survival-verified batches enter DOVU for carbon & biodiversity credit issuance

Beekeeping Enterprise

10 Langstroth hives inside Oloolua managed by trained youth in professional apiculture. Target: 200 kg honey/year at KES 600/kg — premium, forest-sourced, traceable. 2026 Revenue: KES 120,000 | ROI: 167%. Future plan: tokenised honey batches on Hedera for forest-origin certification.

Networking & Collaboration

  • Jaza Miti & AFR100: Active contributors to Kenya's 15 Billion Trees by 2032 and Africa's 100M hectare restoration commitment
